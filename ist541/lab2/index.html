How to Get Started with Mural

By Julia Heatherwick

Mural is an online visual collaboration tool located at www.mural.com. Work with your team in a dynamic virtual environment. Create interactive meetings and support contributions from everyone located anywhere and in any timezone. You will need to learn a few basic features to get started. Mural is performs best using the current version of Google Chrome or Firefox.        

Step 1: Zoom In and Out of Your Mural Canvas

The first step is learning how to zoom in and out of the canvas. The zoom settings are located at the bottom right of your Mural canvas. You can click on Zoom Settings to see your options available.


Mouse: Move your cursor over the area on the canvas you wish to zoom in or out. Using the roller ball of your mouse, scroll up with your index finger to zoom out and scroll down with your index finger to zoom in.



Trackpad: Move your cursor over the area on the canvas you wish to zoom in or out. For Google Chrom users, you can pinch your thumb and index finger to zoom in. Spread your thumb and index finger apart on the trackpack to zoom out. For Firefox users, hold down the Alt key while you slide your two fingers up and down on the trackpad.   

feedback It important to remember to set your zoom settings to match how you move your cursor. If you use a computer mouse, select mouse. If you use a laptop with a trackpad, select trackpad. Click zoom settings again to collapse the settings window.  




Step 2: Move the Canvas or Edit the Canvas

Move the Canvas: Place your cursor over the hand icon and click on it. It is located in the lower right hand corner next to the mini-map. Your cursor will turn into a hand. You can move the canvas around to view a different location. A Mural canvas can get filled up quickly with images and sticky notes. You will want to easily move about the canvas to explore it.   








Edit the Canvas: Place your cursor over the hand icon and click on it again. Your cursor will turn into a pointer. The editing features will appear on the left side of the mural canvas. You can now use the editing features.

feedback  You can easily toggle from the Edit Mode to the Move Mode by pressing down on the space bar as you move your mouse. Release the space bar to continue editing your canvas. Place two fingers on your trackpad to scroll around the Mural canvas. When you are in edit mode you will see a collumn of tools appear on the far left side of the canvas. You can begin adding content to the canvas.




Step 3: Add a Sticky Note

Click on the Sticky Note Icon:  In the edit mode, a tool bar will appear on the far left side of the canvas. Place your cursor over the sticky note icon and click on it. A window will expand and provide elements for you to add to the canvas. You can add text, 3x3 sticky notes, 5x3 sticky notes, or circle sticky notes to the canvs.


Select a Sticky Note: Place your cursor over the blue sticky note (feel free to choose your favorite color) and click on it. Keep your cursor clicked down on the sticky note and then drag it over to the canvas. You may wish to drag over multiple sticky notes.   

feedback If you don't see the tools on the left side of the canvas it means you are not in edit mode. Revisit Step 2 and review how to return to edit mode.


Step 4: Type Inside the Sticky Note

Type into the Sticky Note: Place your cursor on the sticky note and click. You can begin typing into the sticky note. You can click outside the sticky note when you are done entering in your text. You can then move your sticky note anywhere on the canvas. 

feedback If your sticky note appears very small then you need to zoom into your canvas. As a reminder, hover over the sticky note and zoom in. Revisit Step 1 for zoom in and zoom out instructions.   


Step 5: Discover More Features Available to You

Click on the Question Mark Icon: Place your cursor over the question mark icon located in the upper right hand side of the Mural canvas. The drop down menu will display a variety of resources available to you to learn more about Mural. I recommend visiting the shortcuts as a way to discover new ways to interact with the canvas.

feedback I recommend practicing your first four steps. As you gain confidence with zooming in and out, moving around the canvas, editing the canvas, and adding sticky notes then begin exploring the additional features. For fun, see if you can find the icons tool located on the left hand side of the canvas. Select an icon that represents you and drag in onto the canvas!
